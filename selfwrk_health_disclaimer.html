Nothing in SelfWrk constitutes, should be interpreted as, or used as a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment from a licensed physician, registered dietitian, certified personal trainer, or any other qualified healthcare professional.

2. Consult Your Doctor First

Before starting any new exercise program or making significant changes to your diet based on information from this App, you should consult with your physician or a qualified healthcare provider, especially if any of the following apply to you:

  • You have a known cardiovascular condition, heart disease, high blood pressure, or circulatory problem
  • You have diabetes, metabolic syndrome, or blood sugar regulation issues
  • You have any musculoskeletal condition, joint problems, bone density issues, or prior injuries
  • You are pregnant, postpartum, or breastfeeding
  • You have a history of disordered eating, an eating disorder, or body dysmorphia
  • You are currently taking prescription medications that may interact with exercise or dietary changes
  • You have been sedentary for an extended period and are returning to exercise
  • You are under 18 or over 65 years of age
  • You have any other condition that a healthcare professional has advised you to monitor

4. Calorie and Nutrition Calculations

SelfWrk uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to estimate your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). These are mathematical estimates based on statistical averages and may not accurately reflect your individual metabolism, hormonal status, medical history, or specific nutritional needs.

Calorie and macronutrient targets provided by the App are starting point estimates only. Individual needs vary significantly based on factors the App cannot account for including medical conditions, medications, hormonal status, gut microbiome, and genetic variation. Work with a registered dietitian for personalized nutrition guidance.

5. Supplement Information

Any supplement information provided in SelfWrk is for general tracking purposes only. SelfWrk does not recommend, endorse, or advise on specific supplement products or dosages. Supplements are not regulated by the FDA in the same manner as prescription drugs. Consult your physician before beginning any supplement regimen.

6. Stop Immediately If

Stop any exercise immediately and seek medical attention if you experience:

  • Chest pain, pressure, or tightness
  • Severe shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Irregular, rapid, or pounding heartbeat
  • Sudden dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting
  • Sharp or sudden joint or muscle pain
  • Numbness or tingling in your limbs
  • Any other symptom that feels unusual or alarming

7. Mental Health

Fitness tracking apps can sometimes contribute to unhealthy relationships with food, exercise, body image, or weight. If you notice signs of obsessive thinking about calories, exercise, or body weight, or if tracking is causing you distress, please speak with a mental health professional. Your wellbeing is more important than any fitness metric.

If you are struggling with disordered eating, contact the National Alliance for Eating Disorders helpline or speak with your healthcare provider.
